SupportingSan Diego 350

volunteer work

San Diego 350 is an inclusive volunteer organization building a movement to prevent the worst impacts of climate change and climate injustice and strives to create a future that supports a livable planet and just society through education and outreach, public policy advocacy, and mobilizing people to take action.

Role: Web Designer/Developer

goal

One of the awareness *what is the word* San Diego 350 put together a site to raise awareness about Sempra Energy and fracking.

Goal was to improve user experience, bring cohesion between pages, give clear direction of what site was about.

challenge

User experience confusing. No clear message, nor what wanted the user to do. CTA buttons were different colors/sizes 2 of which were title more as menu items than calls to action. No design. Just looked thrown together. No cohesion between pages. Cannot tell on same site.

No hierarchy. Where does user begin to read? Why would reader want to read more? Turns out the ‘raise the alarm’ with megaphone icon is the logo for fracking awareness. Logo begs user to click on it hoping for sound to be produced.

setting the mood

chose the black/grey/white/orange/teal scheme to: 1. Give cohesiveness throughout the site 2. The orange gives the viewer the feeling of ‘warning’ in general. I feel that the subject matter is something to be warned about – therefore having the viewers want to take action or join the cause. Though there is only a dabble of orange, the brightness makes a statement. Test audience felt red was too bold. 3. The teal gives just enough pop to distinguish from the stark black and white. 4. Sections with black background are areas that grabs attention, with calmer grey and white backgrounds for the reader to mellow in order to read more. 5. Black and white header images depict the grimness of the world with fracking. While color images within the pages make an impact by bringing to attention what could happen if fracking continues.
