challenge

Students were tasked with the redesign of ETi's existing website, whose issues included an overload of text, disorganized content, and difficulty in locating and discerning the content and value of ETi's offerings.

original site

objective

The objective was to create a design that would entice users to explore the courses of study via not only clear and concise descriptions but a pleasing visual experience paired with an intuitive, user-friendly navigational path to provide the user an exciting, fruitful experience.

striving to win

The top 10 designs were chosen and individual teams formed to arrive at a winning, final product. It was a competition designed to get students' creative juices flowing -- and a real-world test of our developing skills.

As a born competitor with a great thirst for learning and artistic leanings, I viewed this assignment with enthusiasm!

early rendition sketches

the process

The first door to unlock was the one into that got us into the collective mind of the site's audience. We investigated the pros and cons experienced by a variety of real-world website users and included astute consideration of ETi's expressed desires. Creating personas served as a key foundational piece to keep the design construct on point and inclusive.

After building the framework, just as in interior design, the visual design to include color palette, recognizes the audience. The team was attuned to the fact that an academia aesthetic differs greatly from a seaside aesthetic, for example. We decided on a color scheme with primarily a gray, white and soft blue with small punches of terra cotta to reflect a clean, contemporary feel.

results are in!

Our team's design won and is ETi's current working site! The design provides two ways to explore ETi's offerings from both employer and student perspectives. The "Employer Experience" section explains Contract Education and ways it can assist employers via customized employee training. The "Student Experience" area provides links to Certificate Programs and to individual classes available with a focus on career advancement.

Two of us on our team coded the whole site. (What can I say, except that I genuinely enjoy coding!).

epilogue

After I was credentialed, ETi has reached out to me in a freelance capacity for some continued maintenance and updates. I created their new logo design that incorporates the existing "star" that is part of the SDCCD (college) district's logo (with whom ETi is affiliated). The analogy is that ETi is an "offshoot" of the college. ETi loved it and are currently using it.

(Another aspect of my artistic side, I enjoy creating logos and once even taught myself how to create my own font design! See P05!)
