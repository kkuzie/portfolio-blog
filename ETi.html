overview

Business of Education

San Diego Community Colleges District’s Employee Training Institute (ETi) provides education programs to students with the ability to earn degrees and skills certificates, as well as assist businesses, nonprofit organizations, and public entities with customized education and training for employees and/or clientele.

In October 2019, we partnered with ETi for a ten-week project to design and code a responsive website.

challenge

Text Overload

Team was tasked with the redesign of ETi's existing website, whose issues included an overload of text, disorganized content, and difficulty in locating and discerning the content and value of ETi's offerings.

Main goal was to provide a professional, un-cluttered environment where potential clients would be enticed to explore the courses of study and training services via not only clear and concise descriptions but a pleasing visual experience paired with an intuitive, user-friendly navigational path.

Voice and Tone:
• Professional, informative
• Conservative, easy (to navigate)
• More current in design

original site

objectives

Order, Symmetry, Usability - Oh My!

The project was to design and code a minimum of four pages that avoided the frustration of muddling through the text-heavy current state of the site. Three main objectives were imperative:

• order and symmetry, as well as logical flow,
• have a properly defined, agreed upon navigation pattern for usability, and
• efficiency

process

Market, Needs, and Competition

Kicking off the project, the team mapped out the project scope and gathered some knowledge about the market and the target audience. For this we used:

• user stories and personas
• needs analysis
• competitor analysis

user interface

Narrowing It Down

We investigated the pros and cons experienced by a variety of real-world website users and included astute consideration of ETi's expressed desires of professional and conservative.

Three designs were presented. Each offered distinct styles:

ONE: Simple with a 50s retro undertone
TWO: Modern with artistic flair
THREE: Traditional and straight forward

Each contributed to guiding the client to understand better what they didn't know they were looking for. Though all three were well-received, in the end the more traditional, conservative design was chosen.

early design wireframe

solution

Efficient Navigation

A minimal, easily navigated interface slimmed down to four main pages solved the initial problem of being overwhelmed by text. Direct links to offered courses simplified the site further.

These efficiencies give the user a clear path of how to find what is needed whether as a student finding courses or as an employer seeking customized training programs.

finale

Lessons Learned

DIVVING THE WORK
This was the first time I led a project from meeting with the client all the way through the coding and launch of the site. At the start of the project, whether it was screen design or coding a page, we would be working on the same sections simultaneously. As we progressed, I learned that assigning sections for each of us to work on then coming together to collaborate not only sped up the process but gave the team a part of the project they could call their own.

Epilogue

Since the initial phase outlined above, ETi has reached out to me in a freelance capacity for some continued maintenance and updates. I have added additional course pages, functionable email form, and created their new logo design that incorporates the existing "star" that is part of the San Diego Community College district logo (with whom ETi is affiliated). The analogy being that ETi is an "offshoot" of the college.
