challenges

Potential clients were experiencing an overload of text, disorganized content, and difficulty in locating and discerning the value of the organization's offerings in the descriptors. The objective was to reorganize the website to entice users to explore clear and concise descriptive material with an intuitive, user-friendly navigational path that provides a pleasant and fruitful experience.

The project also included design of an appealing logo.

design & development process

personas

The research phase of this kind of project requires getting into the heads of site users to investigate what works and what could work betterto meet the needs of a variety of individuals. Creating personas serves as important reference to keep the design on point and inclusive.

out of the head, onto paper

I collaborated with a team to create a navigation pattern that allows clients to easily manuever the site's path and flow, easily leading them to view ETi services as well as audiences.

The team created two ways to explore the company's offerings from both employer and student perspectives. The "Employer Experience" section explains Contract Education and ways it can assist employers via customized employee training. The "Student Experience" area provides links to Certificate Programs and individual classes available with a focus on career advancement.

out with old, in with the new

In the case with ETi, the client requested a revamp to something lighter and more contemporary in feel. For the logo design, incorporating the existing "star" that is part of the SDCCD (college) district's logo (with whom ETi is affiliated) was offered as a potential solution, with the analogy that ETi is an offshoot of the college -- or shooting star of sorts.
