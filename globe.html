Rockin'Globe Records

an imagined enterprise

Globe Records operates under the principle that all musical creativity deserves a chance to develop. It isn't about one audience or current trends. The focus is on the unique message each artist wants to relay through their music. It's about individuality and inclusiveness for all genres, whether existing or an entirely brand new concept. It's about reaching out and touching all.

Globe Record's mission is to find and polish talented "diamonds in the rough" to make their originality shine.

beginnings

For this project, I started by creating an eye-pleasing logo -- and went on to build a successful record-producing empire (in my head, at least)! Hang tight while I take you through the myriad stages and components of my design and branding process.

brainstorming

Once I understand a client's personality, I doodle. It's part of my brainstorming technique, my own kind of hieroglyphics or pictoral script, if you will. I write words that I associate with the client and their company goals and then sketch ideas that pop into my head. Over time, the doodles change, are refined, some even discarded or replaced in the process of learning more about the company via discussions or topical research on a product.

opinions matter

The goal of a company's logo is to make a pleasing, memorable visual impact as a long-lasting identifier. For the imaginary Globe Records, things began to take shape once the gold and platinum color palette was added. (I mean, it is the record industry where the ultimate prize is to go gold or platinum!)

This decision illustrates the importance of hearing others' opinions and collaboration. In this case, it was a fellow graphic designer who suggested gold and silver and "Bingo!", when it's right you know it!

(Thank you to my friend, May!)

signage

Building signage must make an impact, especially in an industry that has so many established entities.

logo animation

How to take a logo to a higher level: Animation!

In today's growing high tech visual and, I must add, competitive world, the ability to add movement is often highly desirable. Good animation draws a viewer's interest and can elicit reactions and appeal to a brand in a way that a "flat" logo cannot. Thus it can be a valuable tool to a client's business if done right.

Software: AfterEffects, Audition, Illustrator, Inkscape, Photoshop, GIMP
