Creatin'Sandy Canvas

non-profit art gallery

Sandy Canvas was a start-up non-profit organization whose altruistic mission was to foster the creation of unique local surf-themed art to sell, with proceeds designated for a variety of charities with a focus on drug awareness programs for surfer and disabled veterans' communities.

Each piece of artwork included a synopsis of the individual artist's personal story, most reflecting a synergy between the beauty and soul of of the sea as portrayed through their art.

the request

The client requested a logo design that would reflect the organization's name and its distinctive artwork that utilized donated surfboards as canvasses for emerging local artists.

objective

Create a logo that embraces the appeal of the quintessential Southern California ocean-centric lifestyle.

process

My research started with taking a good look at local art gallery logos to get a feel for what existed.

With my mind set on a sophisticated design that would characterize the client's vision and an emphasis on the chosen medium (the surfboards), I began to jot down my ideas.

I started by creating a mood board in soft, neutral colors and blue hues from which both my logo and web design evolved, a tableau designed to evoke feelings of a day at the beach.

The font choices are Antro-vectra and ETH. The former because of its simplicity and the client's desire for a look that one might find on a California license plate design.

results

After considering several oceanside elements, my mind was drawn to nature's art in the curves of ocean waves that are so enticing to surfers and beach lovers in general. The logo's initials of Sandy Canvas are a subtle reflection of those curves while the surfboard standing in the sand provides a more literal element, all together a synergistic reflection of the client's name.
